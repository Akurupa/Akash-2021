Experienced Leadership

I'm no stranger to taking on leadership roles at Emory and managing teams. In my time at Emory, I've been a tour guide, an Emory Conversation Project Intern, a Peer Health Partner (PHP), a Sophomore Advisor, a Career Ambassador Coordinator, and a member of the Astronomy Club's Executive Board. Most notably, I've served as the President of the Residence Hall Association (RHA) for the past year and had previously been a member of RHA's Programming Committee as Vice President of Dooley's Bowl.

Being the RHA President has given me the most experience with student government, allowing me to serve on the executive boards of both SGA and the 65th College Council.

As RHA President, I've made great strides in improving the organization and making tangible contributions to the student experience. I have

  • Overseen and led all Hall Council prsidents and Executive Board members
  • Bought concrete amenities for each Residence Hall, including foosball tables, Nintendo Switches, and table tennis tables
  • Rewritten the governing documents to increase the organization's efficiency and clarity
  • Revamped and written the weekly RHA Newsletter and organized merch giveaways for both the Atlanta and Oxford communities (while also providing wholesome content and surprise rickrolls)
  • Created Dooley's Tavern, offering a student-led community hub to help transitioning first-years and everyone else stranded at home build and sustain friendships during distance learning
  • Worked to create and oversee Emory Rants, a platform for centralized advocacy for all students

TL;DR: I've done a lot as RHA President, and it's time for me to use that experience and my familiarity with the student experience to lead and improve College Council.
